Water is Life

Water Conservation Systems:

  1. Rainwater Harvesting

    2. Collecting and storing rainwater from rooftops or surfaces for reuse in irrigation, cleaning, or even potable use after treatment.

  2. Greywater Recycling

    3. Reusing water from sinks, showers, and laundry for landscaping or toilet flushing, reducing freshwater demand.

  3. Dual Flush Toilets

    4. Toilets with two flush options—light for liquid waste and full for solid waste—to reduce water usage per flush.

  4. Drip Irrigation

    5. A method where water drips slowly to the plant roots, minimizing evaporation and water waste in agriculture.

  5. Sprinkler Irrigation

    6. Distributes water through a system of pipes and sprinklers, mimicking rainfall and ensuring even water distribution.

  6. Mulching

    7. Applying organic or inorganic materials to soil surfaces to retain moisture, regulate temperature, and reduce evaporation.

  7. Rainwater Storage Tanks

    8. Large tanks that store harvested rainwater for household or agricultural use during dry periods.

  8. Dams

    9. Structures built across rivers to store large volumes of water for irrigation, drinking, flood control, and power generation.

  9. Farm Ponds

    10. Small water reservoirs constructed on farms to store runoff or rainwater for irrigation and livestock.

  10. Water Recycling Plant

    11. Treatment facilities that purify wastewater so it can be safely reused for industrial, agricultural, or even potable purposes.

  11. Terracing

    12. Creating step-like fields on slopes to reduce runoff and increase water infiltration into the soil.

  12. Contour Bunding

    13. Building barriers along land contours to reduce soil erosion and improve water absorption in agricultural lands.

  13. Leak Detection System

    14. Technology used to identify and fix water leaks in pipelines to prevent wastage and ensure system efficiency.

  14. Smart Water Meter

    15. Digital meters that monitor water usage in real-time, helping users detect leaks and track consumption patterns.

  15. Nala Plugging

    16. Blocking small streams (nala) using local materials to slow down water flow and promote groundwater recharge.
