Water Conservation Systems:

  1. Rainwater Harvesting

    2. Captures rain from rooftops and open surfaces into tanks or underground reservoirs for later use in irrigation, cleaning, or filtered domestic supply.

  2. Greywater Recycling

    3. Reuses water from sinks, showers, and laundry for non-potable purposes like watering gardens and flushing toilets.

  3. Dual Flush Toilets

    4. Use two flush modes: a low-volume flush for liquid waste and a full-volume flush for solid waste, helping reduce daily water use significantly.

  4. Drip Irrigation

    5. A precise method that delivers water drop by drop directly to plant roots, reducing evaporation and saving up to 60% of water compared to traditional methods.

  5. Sprinkler Irrigation

    6. Uses pipes and rotating nozzles to simulate rainfall, distributing water evenly across lawns or fields. Best for large open areas.

  6. Mulching

    7. Applies organic or synthetic material on soil to retain moisture, lower soil temperature, prevent weed growth, and reduce evaporation.

  7. Rainwater Storage Tanks

    8. Large above or below ground containers used to store harvested rainwater. Helps communities during drought or low water supply periods.

  8. Dams

    9. Massive structures built to store water for drinking, irrigation, flood control, and hydroelectric power generation.

  9. Farm Ponds

    10. Small ponds constructed on farms to collect and store rainwater or runoff, used for crop irrigation and livestock needs.

  10. Water Recycling Plant

    11. Advanced treatment plants purify used water, making it safe for reuse in industries, agriculture, or even household applications.

  11. Terracing

    12. Creates level steps on hill slopes to reduce runoff and promote water absorption into the soil, especially in hilly farming areas.

  12. Contour Bunding

    13. Soil bunds (barriers) are constructed along the contour lines of land to slow down water flow and promote infiltration.

  13. Leak Detection System

    14. Monitors pipelines and plumbing systems to identify leaks early and prevent water waste in homes, industries, and municipalities.

  14. Smart Water Meter

    15. Digital devices that track and display real-time water consumption, helping users monitor usage, detect overuse, and reduce waste.

  15. Nala Plugging

    16. Traditional method of plugging seasonal streams using local materials like soil, stones, or boulders to slow water flow and recharge groundwater.
