Water Conservation Systems:

  1. Rainwater Harvesting

    2. Captures rain for irrigation, cleaning, or domestic use.

  2. Greywater Recycling

    3. Reuses water from household activities for non-potable needs.

  3. Dual Flush Toilets

    4. Low and full flush modes reduce daily water usage.

  4. Drip Irrigation

    5. Water delivered directly to plant roots, saving up to 60%.

  5. Sprinkler Irrigation

    6. Simulates rainfall for even distribution in large areas.

  6. Mulching

    7. Keeps soil moist, lowers temperature, and prevents weed growth.

  7. Rainwater Storage Tanks

    8. Essential for drought resilience and seasonal water storage.

  8. Dams

    9. Provide water storage, power, and flood control.

  9. Farm Ponds

    10. Store water for irrigation and livestock on farms.

  10. Water Recycling Plant

    11. Purifies used water for industrial and domestic reuse.

  11. Terracing

    12. Reduces runoff and improves soil water absorption.

  12. Contour Bunding

    13. Slows water flow and improves groundwater recharge.

  13. Leak Detection System

    14. Detects pipeline leaks to prevent water waste.

  14. Smart Water Meter

    15. Monitors usage in real-time to curb overconsumption.

  15. Nala Plugging

    16. Uses natural materials to slow stream flow and recharge aquifers.
